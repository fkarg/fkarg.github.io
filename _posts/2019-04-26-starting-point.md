In the ultimate game of life, not even our starting points are equal.

## Starting equally

You know in (tabletop) games - think e.g. Monopoly - in the beginning everyone starts off being equal. In just about every aspect, even. You all start on the same tile, with the same amount of resources (money), and no one thinks that having an earlier turn increases your chances of winning, since otherwise it would be seriously fought over as well. So yes - in the beginning all things are equal.

And then you make your first moves, start to plan, strategize, politicise, control and manipulate - even if only your own pieces. Anyways, at some point, even though everyone started off equally (at least from the perspective of the game), _someone_ is going to win. At some point, if the board hasn't been flipped over, someone _won_. This is clearly a difference to the beginning, where everyone was equal.

This is the same with card games. Take just about every game, really. You start off being 'equal', and usually someone, be it a team or a single person, ends up winning or losing. Sometimes both.


## Starting inequally

Now take the case where next to you a group of people is in some kind of game for some time now, and for some reason or another, one of them has to leave, they ask you to play in \[this persons\] stead, and you do. Now, this is also a starting point. Is it still an 'equal' one? Depending on the game played and how far it has progressed, it would rarely be one. Usually, trends can be seen, rough indications about where everyone stands relative to each other. Also, quite a few things might be decided already, or 'kind of' set in stone. Examples here would be Monopoly, who already owns which streets, or chess, which figures have been taken off the board already.

But then again, here you go. This is your starting point. Complaining won't do you any good, since the one who got you in this situation already left. So the only thing left is to make the best of it. And will you?

## Reevaluating

Usually, you do so to the best of your abilities. You at least try make the best out of the situation, since, you kind of want to _win_, too. Depending on the game played, it might be too late for this already. But you do so anyways. You completely analyze the situation in front of you, evaluate your choices, and act accordingly. Will it be the best action? Maybe. At least according to your knowledge.

Will you play better than the one who left earlier? Will you be able to build upon what he started, to notice his intricate design of the current situation in front of you? This usually depends on you, on the skill you have at this game, on the _level_ you play this game at.

Consider the following; a _really long_ running game, with teams. One of the teams gets to regularly exchange their players (even if the others still could have gone on), whilst the other does not. All else being equal (let it be some kind of [strategic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Game_classification) game), which team do you think is more likely to win?






Do you know the rules? Do you _understand_ the game?
