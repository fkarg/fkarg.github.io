If your idea is sufficiently powerful, you can probably write a book about it.
Actually, anything less will probably not sufficient.


* TOC
{:toc}


So I had this idea recently, that if you have an idea that is sufficiently
profound, you can probably write a book about it. Or, more precisely: You need
at least one full book to explain it in detail. What do I mean by that?


Consider living your life, being interested in a bunch of topics. And one day,
strolling through a shopping mall it just hits you. Some severely profound
connection no one seems to be aware of. The whole world just kind of seems to make
sense, since reality offered you one more layer of perception. Now, it might
not be the shopping mall. Might be a library, or while browsing the net.

So you have this newfound principle and want to share it with the world. You
might start by talking about it with your friends. They don't get your
explanation at first, since your understanding is based on unusual
methodologies. But after a few attempts of trying ot explain it, they seem to
get it, and their eyes light up. That means: you really might be unto
something.

You decide to write about it. On a blog post maybe. So you start writing. You
get some friends to look ove at first, maybe your current life companion. They
give you valuable feedback and you start to incorporate it. You refine the idea
and research more about the origins. You start to get a much more profound
understanding, and want to dwelve deeper into these parts. At some point they
develop to fundamental ideas you need to present in what you are writing about,
all the while having a clear focus on understandability and trying to give
examples wherever possible.

Inbetween, you notice that there are parts to it. So you write a rough
introduction and outline first. Some parts later can be neatly seperated, let's
call them chapters. And suddenly, what you have in front of you is a book.
Probably not a thousand pages. Maybe a few hundred, certainly a few dozen, if
you were thorough.

Profound principles, for whatever reason, require that much content to be fully
understood. No, to be more accurate: even with that much context building,
putting it in perspective and explaining it with stories, it usually is not
enough to convey the full force of the explained principle in all it's details.
But that is okay. It is important to convey the core idea in it's various
forms, because you will become aware of most of the implications yourself, as
time will go on, with open eyes. Since, after all, you've been shown one more
layer of reality. When being perceptive about it, it is just a matter of time
at this point, for you to unravel your own version, your own principle.

While you still used the original idea, the original principle, after
incorporating it in your Being, after having let some of the implications
propagate, is when you receive the most value. But all the connections that are
made, in full specificity, mentally or otherwise, would fill more than just one
book. But you are happy with the book, since it contains the core idea. You are
now an author, an 'authority' on this principle, on this layer of reality.
