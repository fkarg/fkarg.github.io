# The Value of a Smartphone

<!-- picture of a smartphone -->

Without knowing the exact percentages, but I think that in todays world most
people prefer to have a smartphone rather than not having one. You can easily
see this by the speed by which people repair or get a new phone, after breaking
it. So apparently a smartphone has value in todays world.

## Smartphones in medieval times
By itself, a smartphone is worthless. Imagine having a smartphone in the middle
ages. Just the phone, no charger or additional solar-powered batteries, as is
common in certain stories. It might be able to entertain you for a bit, but it
will be worthless after the battery dies out. Even if you know a bit of
electrical engineering, and that chargers usually have 5 Volt, just _how much
is 5V_? Even if you manage to build a water wheel providing you with the
electrical power, do you know between which contacts you need to apply the 5V
to? Even with that out of the way, some recent smartphones require a data
connection to the charger to allow them to charge the phone. At some point, it
will be a black mirror only. A smartphone provides little value outside of a
modern context.

But based on that we can see one thing: The value of a smartphone is related
quite a bit based on the context/world it is in. So what Value does it provide?

## In recent times
According to [Jung, Yoonhyuk (2013)][smartphone-value], socialization, an
improvement of communication, a productive daily life and the aquiring of
information as well es restorative relaxing (by playing games or the like) are
the main values people obtain from smartphones. I would assume that this did
not change significantly in the recent years. The most significant change would
probably be the rise of the value obtained from fitness- and health-related
trackers.

## Smartphones without connection
So thinking about this for a moment, we realize that almost all of the value
obtained from a smartphone comes from the communication a smartphone has to the
world. This contains a productive daily life, the aquiration of information and
partially playing games. The other part, socialization and improvement of
communication can only be obtained if other people have smartphones as well.

The core essence being: basically all of the value we obtain from smartphones
are due to network effects, effects where the smartphone is only a medium and
does not provide the value itself, only making it available.

## The Data Engine
We can see network effects (providing more value when more people use it) in
certain other everyday applications. The biggest one are probably recommendations
of online services, be it the songs on spotify, the search results on google,
or the products on amazon. They can only provide the value they do because they
can base their recommendations on the interactions of hundreds, thousands,
sometimes millions of people.
