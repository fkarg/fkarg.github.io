I've been reading a bunch of chinese novels in the last few years. I noticed
some common trends among them, and just wanted to share them with you.
Particularly those, that might seem ... weird from a western perspective at
first.

* TOC
{:toc}


## Strength as righteousness
It is the most apparent in wuxia-style novels, as strength is really taken as a
justification to do something - be it physical, economical, or through
connections. This is blatantly apparent in most confrontations with 'evil'
guys, where a common line tends to be something among the lines of 'you have
only your weakness to blame' (你只有自己的缺点要怪). And yes, this as a
justification for _everything_ the 'evil' guys will try.

## Loyalty to those who deserve it
Even if the Main Character (MC) will mainly act alone and not be under some
sort of master, they tend to be extremely loyal to their close and true
friends. These friends however, tend to be carefully selected, and ascertained
of their personality before the loyality is shown or required.

## Cars, Villas, ...



- Strength as righteousness
- Loyalty
- Revenge
- Friendship
- Selfishness
- Pettiness
- Cars, Villas, high-status-connections -> status
- Considerable strain to 'upgrade' yourself
- Value of close Family (long lost)
- Generousity to good people and none to bad ones
- Scheming against each other
- Convoluted conversations
- A lot of idioms
- People thinking they are 'worth' more, exercising power
- Helping out Family and Friends with own Success
- when getting stronger, it is imperative to get rid of 'impurities' within the body
- 'keeping face', and the surrounding idioms
- portrayel of evil person: doted on by someone and thus strength without having put in the 'actual' work -> failing against mc
