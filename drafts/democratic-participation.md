in democracies, people need to have ways to take on responsibility to ensure
that the government does what they want it to for it to remain stable


why does simple voting not suffice:
- voting doesn't happen often (only every 4/5/8/N years)
    - benefit when someone good was voted
    - severe negative when a voted official suddenly isn't doing what the citizens want (andrew cuomo)
- a lot of energy is spent on signaling instead of making good decisions
- there is a number of cases where lobbying _seems_ to have had influence
  effects (potentially counter to what society would benefit from)
- it's not fine-grained influence on what happens
- most commonly-used voting systems allow for strategic voting (voting for a
  bigger party instead of your preferred candidate)
