From Tim Ferris' four-hour-workweek (p46-47):
> 1. Define your nightmare, the absolute worst that could happen if you did what you are considering.
> 2. What steps could you take to repair the damage or get things back on the upswing, even if temporarily?
> 3. What are the outcomes or benefits, both temporary and permanent, of more probable scenarios?
> 4. If you were fired from your job today, what would you do to get things under financial control?
> 5. What are you putting off out of fear?
> 6. What is it costing you - financially, emotionally, and physically - to postpone action?
> 7. What are you waiting for?



From Tim Ferris' four-hour-workweek (p57-62):
> 1. What would you do if there were no way to fail? Maybe two timelines
>    6mo/12mo, list five things of _having_ (might be material wants), _being_
>    (great cook, fluent in chinese), and _doing_ in that order. Consider what
>    you hate/fear and write down the opposite. Don't care _how_ they happen,
>    and don't fool yourself.
> 2. In case of blank: a) What would you do having 100mio in the bank? b) What
>    would make you most excited to wake up to? If still blank for doing:
>    - place to visit
>    - thing to do before you die
>    - thing to do daily / weekly
>    - always wanted to learn ...
> 3. Convert _beings_ to _doings_ that would characterize success in _being_.
> 4. From everything, highlight the four most exciting things for 6mo timeline
> 5. Determine costs of everything for Target Monthly Income to Target Daily Income
> 6. Determine _three steps_ for eas of the four dreams, do the first now. If
>    it cannot be done in 5minutes, break it down. If you can't do it now,
>    schedule it as the first thing tomorrow morning.
> Goal: Endgoal, TMI, and momentum with first steps done. Do the other two
> tomorrow and the day after tomorrow (before 11am each)
> In case of research: Ask someone who did the same before you instead of
> paralysis by analysis
