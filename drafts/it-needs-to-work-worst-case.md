similarity in the following books:
- Mark Manson in [Everything is F\*cked][everything-fcked]
- Jordan Peterson in [Twelve Rules for Life][12-rules]
- Charles Duhigg in [Power of Habit][power-habit]

all have the requirement to work/be believable in the _worst_ situation imaginable.

This and the implications

some ideas and impplications from my side

- personally
    makes sense, suffering catches up eventually, and it all depends on how you
    fare then -> needs more fleshing out
- in a team
    groups have ups and downs as well, and to work properly, all processes need
    to still do their intended function when everything fails, close to wanting
    to do harm intentionally
- in a society
    there will always be those on the bottom of society, and how well a society
    works depends a lot on how well these people is being helped
    -> search for studies and stuff
