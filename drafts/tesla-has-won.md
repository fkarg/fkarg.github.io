Common objections:
- build quality is low: used to be true, they are now world-leading
- but everything is electric and software: so you noticed how bad the others are. But seriously, it's awesome
- but they're not safe?: -> impact report, much safer than other cars (on best available comparison)
- I would never use autopilot: Sure, then don't.


Reasonable objections:
- They won't be able to get enough batteries: reasonable assumption, but
  they've been working on it for years now, including the supply chain. Sure,
  it's bound to be a challenge, but not an unsurmountable one.
- They're too expensive: while this is true, it is going to change in the
  future. a lot of people buy cars second-hand, and this is going to drive
  costs down over time as well.
- They're involved with many lawsuits that sound real bad: yeah but consider it
  in perspective; that's the same for every other large company as well.
- They didn't even really make profits so far: Yeah, because that isn't their goal
    -> isn't it a bad company/investment then? Sure, feel free to not invest.
- china could get real problematic if they continue like this: true, but I
  don't think this'll happen. Give it a bit of time, and a lot of people will
  change their mind, there's nothing fundamentally going on against them
- what about progress in eroupe? Sure, this'll slow them down a few months, but
  that's not going to be enough for anyone to catch them
