# Content
Big Five For Life is a fictional story about Thomas Derale, who built a
business empire and is loved by everyone. In this story, he is the progenitor
for a number of interesting concepts, diverging from 'common managerial
wisdom'. You can find a number of these concepts listed at
[Takeaways](#main-takeaways).

Thomas is dying, is among the first information the book is telling us. Later
on, we get to know it is due to cancer. Most of the story happens in the time
just before his death, with frequent flashbacks or stories told of the past.

Generally, the focus is on exploring the style of leadership Thomas cultivated
in his companies and arouond himself. We get introduced to his wife, a number
of key executives from his companies, as well as reception clerks -- here
called 'director of first impressions'.

The fundamental reason for Thomas's success seems to stem from his approach of
aligning everyone's purpose for existing. There's also less micromanagement and
other 'nontraditional' very effective leadership approaches, which, when put
together supercharges everything.
