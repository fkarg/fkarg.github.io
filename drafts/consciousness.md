Why I would argue that we are not conscious most of the time, and what to do about that.




Are you conscious right now? Have you ever **not** been conscious? What does it
mean to be conscious in the first place?


Are you conscious during sleep? If you argue that conscious is just the
electrochemical reactions in the brain happening, then sure, you are conscious
as long as you are alive. Consciousness, at least the way I think about it, is
more about the quality of choices we make than something else. It's the feeling
of being capable of facing whatever challenge pops up, head first. Being calm
and decisive, at least mentally, while also making good decisions. Moments of
being fully in control.


There is an arbitrarily large focus on making choices and facing problems here.
This is partially due to the lack of better words. I will get back to that
later.
