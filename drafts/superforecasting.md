# Review

## Key Takeaways / Ten Commandments for Aspiring Superforecasters
### Triage
- use on Problems decently far out but not too far (12 years is not reasonable)
- and the right kind of problem, where not just an fermi-estimate will suffice
### Break Bigger Problems Down Into Tractable Sub-Problems
### Strike the Right Balance Between Inside And Outside Views
### Strike the Right Balance Between Under- and Overreacting to Evidence
### Look for Clashing Causal Forces at Work on Each Problem
- for every good policy, there's an exception
### Strive to Distinguish as many degrees of doubt as the problem permits
### Strike the Right Balance Between Under- and Overconfidence, between prudence and decisiveness
### Look for the errors behind your mistakes but beware of hindsight bias
### Bring out the best in others and let others bring out the best in you
### master the error-balancing bycicle
- learning requires doing
    - frequent, fast, good feedback
    - deliberate practice
### Don't treat commandments as commandments
- break the rules every now and then


### Fermi-ize: outside view first
### also consider inside view
### consider multiple different models / viewpoints
### update on new information appropriately
### exchange information with others
