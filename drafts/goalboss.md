## Criticism
Damn narcissist, telling a lot of different stories. I heard Will Pemble talk
about 'Death by Winning' as a speaker at CloudFest2019. There, he told a
different, and honestly, more believable, more human story. But I included the
details about that there already.

Overall, I learned a couple of things and will probably implement some of the
recommended actions as often as possible. It is clear that the focus is on
_team_ productivity, and as such, is the first of this topic that I have read.

It compliments quite well with [Getting Things Done](../getting-things-done/),
since it focuses exclusively on personal productivity.

# Chapters
## 01 Death by Winning
- built web.com from own basement, as well as two other companies (and exited)
- he has now multiple homes all over the world and logs about 45 days of family vacation every year
- this was not always the case
- starting out with web.com they became so successful, they could not keep up with customer demand

- three main domains they were lacking in:
    - Communication. They barely communicated and barely anyone knew what someone else was doing
    - Delegation. No rest as the CEO, without him, everything would crash and burn instantly
    - Time Management. Only reacting to problems, no time for anything else, certainly not personal improvement

- 'living the dream', getting crushed by success

> That's when I decided. Enough! I'm going to do this, this and that!

- There is no such hero-like happenings. What he told in a keynote:
    - one of their investors, a good friend of his, was about to withhold money, if he did not go to a entrepreneur seminar
    - so he did
    - and it was the best fucking thing that happened to him

- 'determined to build leadership and management system'. Yeah right

- Steps:
    - implementing key metrics, and goals for them (as a team)
    - most people did not like it (of course, it was entirely different)
    - it started to generate results, consistently

- biggest realization: could work for other companies as well
- sold web.com and was invited to share experience
- this system allowed for far lower stress and much more family time

> When no one is in charge, everyone is in charge. What a terrible way to build a business!
