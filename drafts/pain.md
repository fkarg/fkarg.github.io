I read alkjash's [Pain is not the unit of effort][original] post a few days
ago, and I felt like something was ... missing while reading it. Not that it's
not true, in fact, I believe the two rules do apply. But ... maybe not in the
way they might be easily interpreted in.

> 1. If it hurts, you're probably doing it wrong.
> 2. You're not trying your best if you're not happy.



The easiest thing to do is to drop down and vegetate. And yet you are reading
this post right now. Why?






Humans are innately curious, wanting to learn things.


- status (money, power, ...)
- the satisfaction of having understood something deeply / the beauty of something
- the satisfaction of creation (tools, food, art, ...)


- mastery
- purpose
- autonomy




fun in games:
- challenge just beyond your level of skill, but clearly doable
- interaction with other humans (social stuff)
- exploration / creativity (seeing new things)
- objectives/goals are clear and seemingly only few steps away
- strategizing/planning the next few steps
- learning new mechanics/conditions/ ... (surroundings?)



among the most motivational: display of incredible competency
