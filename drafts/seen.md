I just want someone to see me. To see the true me. To see me for who I am, the
full me. I think everyone desires that.

I also want to see some people. Some more so than others. They also want to be
seen. Everyone wants to be seen. Everyone wants to be interesting, having
others interested in them or at least what they do.

There is nothing wrong with this, I think. It's just very paradoxical, because
we don't really want to see others - not for who they really are. Any yet we
want to be seen. Why?

Part of it is trust. You can trust someone to understand you. To understand
you, even when you make a mistake. To love you for whatever you do - since
whatever you do wouldn't change anything in their view. It doesn't in mine,
after all.

Or can you trust someone like that? What stops you from just telling someone
everything about you? It's the trust you have right now - what will this person
do with this information? Will they use it against me in the future? I know
someone who will. I know others who won't, but I've been through a lot with them.

Is that it? Having been through something troubling with someone? Having built
trust? Having shared weaknesses, without treason or betrayal. Trusting in the
integrity of the person, even when pressured - not possible, without seeing how
they will act under pressure.

Who would you call, if you had a body to bury? Why would you call them? Would
they call you too? Why would they call you, and not someone else?

I just want to be seen. Understood. Valued. Not superficially. Sure, that's fun
too. But it's too easy to lose yourself. It's too cheap, too powerful, and will
not satisfy you. Not for long, anyways.

Smart and famous people don't want to be seen too much. Because they're not
really being seen for who they are, most of the time. Reporters just chase the
next headline - but that's not the full story, not who you are, not who you
want to be.

I don't want to be famous. I want to be recognized. Recognized for what I can
contribute. Recognized for what I can do, what I am capable of. Even though
it's not much.

I want to be a good friend. Some say that I'm too nice. But maybe I'm just
afraid to be myself.

Someone said I was authentic once. I tried to be more of that. I don't think I
succeeded.
